Common Symptoms of Vitiligo

White or light patches on the skin

Common on hands, face, and around body openings

Early signs may start as small pale spots

Hair on affected areas may turn white or grey

Patches may grow or spread over time

Often no pain or irritation, just visible skin changes

Causes of Vitiligo

Autoimmune Response
The immune system attacks pigment-producing cells

Genetics
Family history increases the risk

Skin Injuries
Cuts, burns, or sunburns may trigger white patches

Stress
Emotional or physical stress can worsen the condition

Chemical Exposure
Contact with certain chemicals may damage melanocytes

Hormonal Imbalance
Changes in hormones may contribute to pigment loss
